Entering Data for your Organisation
Entering Gas Data
If you have gas sources there are two ways of entering the data. This is done manually or by uploading a file containing the data (generally a csv file). If you just have a single value of how much gas you have used for each month you should enter your data manually. If you have a list of hourly meter readings provided by your utility company or by a smart meter than you should upload a file.*
Manual Entry of Gas Data
This guide will show you how to upload gas data manually, we use the terminology gas reading to refer to a single piece of data which states how much gas was used between two dates.

To enter gas data manually click on the menu Upload > Enter Gas Data
Fig.2 Manual Entry of Gas Data

Next click on the Enter New Gas Reading button

Fig.3 Enter New Gas Reading Button

*This section will not apply if your organisation has no meters or sources

This will bring you to the form to enter your Gas Readings

Fig.4 Gas Readings Form
  • Gas Value: This is the amount of gas that was used during your billing period. You will find this on your gas bill.
  • Start Date: Select from the date picker the first day your gas reading refers to. This would be the date on your gas bill when the billing period starts.
  • End Date: Select from the date picker the last day your gas reading refers to. This would be the date on your gas bill when the billing period ends.
  • Units: This is the units that your gas usage is measure in. This will be stated on your gas bill.
  • User Log: Select the meter that this reading is coming from. If this list is empty you need to refer to the Configuring Meters section.

Once you have filled everything out click on the Save button.

This will bring you back to the page listing all gas readings which have been created, with a message stating the successful creation of your gas reading.
